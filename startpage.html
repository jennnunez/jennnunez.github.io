Our Ecological Footprint

What is an Ecological Footprint?

An ecological footprint is the impact a person, or group of people, are making to the environment.

It measures the amount of natural resources we use in order to sustain our lifestyles.

For example, the amount of water you use when you shower/bathe, and how the overuse of clean water will soon lead to there being no clean water at all.

Why is knowing this important to learn?

The environment is made up of your surroundings: the plants, animals, and people around you.

It is important to learn how your daily routines are helping to improve or harm the environment.

When you do not take care of your environment, you are harming not only yourself, but everything and everyone else who surrounds you.

Like the example above, once all the clean water is gone, there will be no water to clean yourself with, to drink, or to use for anything else.

However, you are still in time to change and do better for the world you live in!!
